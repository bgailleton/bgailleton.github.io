LSDTopoTools is a research software for topographic analysis. It's a set of high performance `C++` routines with some `python` for the visualisation aiming explicitely at making topographic analysis reproducible and objective. Started in 2013, Simon M. Mudd is the lead developer at the University of Edinburgh

I worked a lot on it during my PhD I added multiple tools including [kinckpoint extraction](https://esurf.copernicus.org/articles/7/211/2019/) and [constraining concavity index](https://esurf.copernicus.org/articles/6/505/2018/).

I made `python` bindings to make both usage and installation easier, CD/CI with conda-forge: [`lsdtopytools`](https://github.com/LSDtopotools/lsdtopytools). 

**Ressources**: https://github.com/LSDtopotools
